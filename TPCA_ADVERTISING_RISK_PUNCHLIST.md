## Summary for counsel

The pattern across every page mirrors what was already flagged on collection.html: the core legal question isn't the sales-ban (well mitigated) but whether any web content displaying tobacco brand names, product imagery, tasting/flavor descriptions, or authenticity/curation claims constitutes "advertising" or "marketing communication" under Art. 30/31, regardless of page purpose (retail, lounge, about, accessories, or even structured SEO schema). The highest-density risk clusters are:

1. about.html's brand/flavor table and accessories.html's branded product grid — the two pages outside collection.html with the most direct brand-name + product-image combinations.
2. Sitewide mega-menu and homepage marquee — because they appear on every single page, any Art. 31 finding here has site-wide reach, not just a single-page fix.
3. Homepage FAQ schema — brand list embedded in structured data, invisible to a casual reader but fully "published."

Recommend counsel prioritize review in that order, since the mega-menu and schema fixes (if required) would be the fastest wins with the broadest risk reduction — a small number of shared components rather than per-page rewrites.

Note: index.html's remaining sections (collections carousel, community, bar, B2B sections) had not been fully read at time of writing — full read recommended before finalizing scope with counsel.
